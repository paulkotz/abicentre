Acquired brain injury
  • Our Service
  • For all Victorians
  • At a Glance

The Acquired Brain Injury (ABI) Rehabilitation Centre is Victoria’s first purpose-built centre of excellence in brain injury rehabilitation.

When it opens in late–2014, the centre will provide care and treatment for patients from the early stages of injury, through to rehabilitation. The ABI Rehabilitation Centre will substantially improve access to quality care for patients with moderate to severe brain injury resulting from traumatic accidents, illness or stroke.

The centre includes a purpose-built 42-bed inpatient unit and community rehabilitation service on the Caulfield Hospital site. A four-bedroom transitional living service is also being constructed near to the hospital in a homely residential setting.
